> The basic concept, Tran said, is that sound waves are also "pressure
> waves, and they displace some of the oxygen" as they travel through
> the air. Oxygen, we all recall from high school chemistry, fuels fire.
> At a certain frequency, the sound waves "separate the oxygen \[in the
> fire\] from the fuel. The pressure wave is going back and forth, and
> that agitates where the air is. That specific space is enough to keep
> the fire from reigniting."
